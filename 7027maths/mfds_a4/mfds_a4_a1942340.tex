\section*{Assignment 4 - Question 1}

Consider $A = \begin{bmatrix}
    a & b\\
    c & d
\end{bmatrix}$, to make $A ^ 2 = \begin{bmatrix}
    4 & -2\\
    0 & 1
\end{bmatrix}$,
we could write an expression below:
\[
\begin{bmatrix}
    a & b\\
    c & d
\end{bmatrix}
\cdot
\begin{bmatrix}
    a & b\\
    c & d
\end{bmatrix}
=
\begin{bmatrix}
    4 & -2\\
    0 & 1
\end{bmatrix}
\]
\[
\begin{bmatrix}
    a^2 + bc & ab + bd\\
    ac + cd & bc + d^2
\end{bmatrix}
= 
\begin{bmatrix}
    4 & -2\\
    0 & 1
\end{bmatrix}
\]

And then we could get the relationships of $a,b,c$ and $d$:

\begin{numcases}{}
a^2 + bc = 4 \\
ab + bd = -2 \\
ac + cd = 0 \\
bc + d^2 = 1
\end{numcases}

from (2) and  (3), we could learn that:
$$
\begin{aligned}
    ac &= -cd \\
    ab &= -2 -bd
\end{aligned}
$$
